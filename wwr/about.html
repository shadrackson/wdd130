Our Rich History

From Humble Beginnings

Whitewater rafting has roots tracing back to the mid-19th century when the first wooden rafts were used for exploration and transport on wild rivers. In 1842, Lieutenant John Fremont and Horace H. Day constructed a rubber raft to navigate the Platte River, laying the foundation for modern rafting.

Evolution to Recreation

By the mid-20th century, rafting evolved from exploration into recreation. With advancements in inflatable rafts after World War II, the sport grew rapidly. Today, whitewater rafting is a popular adventure activity worldwide, combining skill, teamwork, and thrill across rivers of varying difficulty.
